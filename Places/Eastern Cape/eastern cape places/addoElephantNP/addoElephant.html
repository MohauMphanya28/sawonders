Addo Elephant National Park is a renowned wildlife conservation area located in the Eastern Cape of South Africa, just outside Gqeberha (formerly Port Elizabeth). Established in 1931 to protect a small group of elephants, the park has grown into one of the country’s largest and most diverse national parks. It's home to the Big 7 — elephant, lion, leopard, rhino, buffalo, whale, and great white shark making it unique among global wildlife reserves. Visitors can explore scenic landscapes through game drives, guided tours, and hiking trails. Known for its conservation efforts and malaria-free environment, Addo is ideal for family getaways, nature lovers, and safari enthusiasts looking to experience Africa’s wildlife up close.
