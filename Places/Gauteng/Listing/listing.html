Relive South Africa's Greatest stories

Constitution Hill

Constitution Hill is a living museum and the seat of South Africa's Constitutional Court, located in Johannesburg. Once a notorious prison complex, it now stands as a symbol of the country's journey from oppression to democracy, honoring the resilience of those who fought for freedom.

Apartheid Museum

he Apartheid Museum in Johannesburg offers a profound exploration of South Africa's history of racial segregation and the nation's journey toward democracy. Through its extensive exhibits, the museum provides visitors with a deep understanding of the apartheid era and its lasting impact on the country.

Union Buildings: Nelson Mandela electorial setting as the first black president of the Republic Of South Africa

Uninon Buildings

The Union Buildings in Pretoria serve as the official seat of the South African government and the President's offices. Designed by Sir Herbert Baker and completed in 1913, the neoclassical structure symbolizes national unity and is surrounded by terraced gardens featuring monuments, including a prominent statue of Nelson Mandela. The site has hosted significant events, such as the 1956 Women's March and Mandela's 1994 presidential inauguration.

The first fossils found in Africa

Cradle of Humankind

The Cradle of Humankind is a UNESCO World Heritage Site located approximately 50 kilometers northwest of Johannesburg, South Africa. Spanning about 47,000 hectares, it encompasses a network of limestone caves that have yielded the world's richest concentration of early hominin fossils, some dating back over 3 million years. Notable discoveries include Australopithecus africanus specimens like "Mrs. Ples" and "Little Foot," as well as Australopithecus sediba and Homo naledi . The site offers educational experiences through attractions like the Maropeng Visitor Centre and the Sterkfontein Caves.

Johhanesburg Zoo

The Johannesburg Zoo is a popular family-friendly attraction located in the heart of Johannesburg, home to over 320 animal species and known for its conservation and education programs.

Rietvlei Nature Reserve

Rietvlei Nature Reserve, located just south of Pretoria, is one of the world's largest urban nature reserves, covering approximately 3,800 hectares. Established in 1948, it encompasses the Rietvlei Dam, which supplies about 15% of Pretoria's drinking water. The reserve is home to diverse wildlife, including white rhinos, buffalo, cheetahs, zebras, and over 240 bird species. Visitors can enjoy activities such as hiking, cycling, horseback riding, fishing, and guided game drives. With its rich biodiversity and recreational offerings, Rietvlei provides a serene escape within the city limits

Walter Sisulu Botanical Garden

The Walter Sisulu National Botanical Garden, located in Roodepoort, Johannesburg, is a 300-hectare reserve established in 1982. Renowned for its diverse flora and fauna, it features over 600 indigenous plant species and approximately 240 bird species, including a nesting pair of Verreaux's eagles near the picturesque Witpoortjie Waterfall. The garden offers tranquil walking trails, picnic spots, and educational facilities, making it a cherished natural retreat for visitors seeking relaxation and nature appreciation.
