  • What To Expect The museum is situated just two blocks from the site where Hector Pieterson was fatally shot. It features a range of exhibits, including:
    Television footage of the Soweto Uprising
    Oral testimonies from participants and witnesses
    Photographs and audiovisual displays
    Historical documents detailing the events of 1976
    Visitors can also view the iconic photograph by Sam Nzima, capturing the moment Hector's lifeless body was carried by fellow student Mbuyisa Makhubo, with Hector's sister Antoinette Sithole running alongside—a powerful image that drew international attention to the brutality of apartheid.​
    The memorial and museum together serve not only as a remembrance of those who lost their lives but also as a celebration of the youth's role in the struggle for freedom.

The Hector Pieterson Memorial and Museum in Orlando West, Soweto, stands as a poignant tribute to the students who bravely protested against apartheid-era education policies during the Soweto Uprising of 16 June 1976. Named after 12-year-old Hector Pieterson, one of the first casualties of the uprising, the museum offers a deep and moving exploration of this pivotal moment in South African history
