  • Powerful Exhibits Over 20 permanent exhibitions using film, photographs, text, and artifacts.
  • Passbook Entry Experience Visitors are randomly assigned an identity (White or Non-White) at the entrance, reflecting the racial classification system of apartheid.
  • Mandela and Tutu Exhibits Displays focusing on key figures like Nelson Mandela and Desmond Tutu.
  • Outdoor Garden of Reflection A quiet space for contemplation after the museum visit.

The Apartheid Museum is one of the most important cultural institutions in South Africa, dedicated to documenting the rise and fall of apartheid. Opened in 2001, the museum provides an emotional and educational journey through the history of racial segregation and the long struggle for freedom and equality in South Africa.
