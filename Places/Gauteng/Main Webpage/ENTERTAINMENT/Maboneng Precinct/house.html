Maboneng Precinct

25 Reviews

Situated in the eastern part of Johannesburg's central business district, Maboneng is easily accessible by car or public transport. It's advisable to visit during daylight hours and remain aware of your surroundings, as with any urban area

Arts & Culture

Arts on Main: The cornerstone of Maboneng's revitalization, this space houses artist studios, galleries, and a weekly food and design market.
The Centre for the Less Good Idea: Founded by renowned artist William Kentridge, this cultural organization supports experimental and collaborative art projects.
David Krut Projects: A gallery and print workshop focusing on contemporary South African art, offering exhibitions and art books.

  • Dining & Nightlife Bertrand Café: A French-inspired café offering a relaxed atmosphere and a menu blending European and African flavors.
    Mama Mexicana: Located within Arts on Main, this eatery serves flavorful Mexican dishes in a minimalist setting.
    The Living Room: An eco-friendly rooftop venue adorned with greenery, offering panoramic city views, cocktails, and live music
  • Shopping & Markets Market on Main: A popular Sunday market featuring local food vendors, designers, and artists, providing a taste of Johannesburg's diverse culture
    Local Boutiques: Explore unique clothing stores, art shops, and craft stalls scattered throughout the precinct, showcasing South African creativity.
  • Street Art & Tours Maboneng is renowned for its vibrant street art, with over 50 murals adorning its buildings. Guided walking tours, such as those offered by MainStreetWalks, provide insights into the area's history, art, and culture.

The Maboneng Precinct, meaning “Place of Light” in Sesotho, is a vibrant urban neighborhood located on the eastern edge of Johannesburg's city center. Since its inception in 2009, it has transformed from a cluster of derelict warehouses into a dynamic hub for creativity, culture, and entrepreneurship..
