Walter Sisulu Botanical Garden

25 Reviews

End of Malcolm Rd, Poortview, Roodepoort, Gauteng, South Africa

Highlights

Witpoortjie Waterfall: A stunning 70-meter waterfall serves as the garden's centerpiece, cascading over the Roodekrans ridge.​
Verreaux’s Eagles: A breeding pair of these majestic birds nests on the cliffs near the waterfall, making this one of the few urban sites in the world where they can be observed in their natural habitat.
Biodiversity: Home to over 600 indigenous plant species and approximately 240 bird species, the garden showcases the rich flora and fauna of the region. ​

Themed Gardens: Explore various sections, including the Cycad Garden, Succulent Rockery, Fern Trail, and Waterwise Garden, each highlighting different plant communities.

  • Picnic Areas Enjoy a relaxing picnic on the expansive lawns.
  • Hiking Trails Several trails, including a 3.5 km route leading to the top of the waterfall, offer opportunities for exploration and exercise
  • Bird Hide A secluded spot for birdwatching enthusiasts to observe various species in their natural environment.
  • Children's Playground A dedicated area with play equipment and a small train ride for young visitors.

The Walter Sisulu National Botanical Garden in Roodepoort, Gauteng, is a tranquil 300-hectare sanctuary that seamlessly blends landscaped gardens with natural Highveld vegetation. Established in 1982, it has become a beloved destination for nature enthusiasts, families, and hikers alike.
