Rietvlei Nature Reserve

Rietvlei Nature Reserve, Pretoria, Gauteng, South Africa

Wildlife & Birdlife

Home to over 1,600 large mammals, visitors can expect to see:
White rhino, buffalo, hippo, cheetah, zebra, eland, red hartebeest, waterbuck, and black wildebeest

Entry fees

Adults (18 years and older): R75​

Children (under 12 years): R32​

Trails Access: R50 per entry or R150 for a 10-entry access card (family sharing permitted)

Operating Hours

Reserve Hours: Daily from 6:00 AM to 6:00 PM.

Picnic Park: Open from 9:00 AM to 5:00 PM

  • Prime Specimen Nocturnal species such as aardvark, bushpig, brown hyena, and serval are often spotted during night drives.
  • Activities & Facilities Self-Drive Safaris: Explore over 30 km of tarred and 10 km of gravel roads at your own pace. ​
    Guided Game Drives & Night Drives: Join scheduled tours to gain deeper insights into the reserve's ecology. ​
    Hiking Trails: Two main trails are available—a 14 km day trail and a 21 km multi-day trail. ​
    Fishing & Non-Motorised Water Sports: Permitted in designated areas of the dam. ​
    Picnic Spots & Bird Hides: Scattered throughout the reserve for relaxation and wildlife observation.

​Rietvlei Nature Reserve, situated just south of Pretoria, offers a serene escape into nature without venturing far from the city. Spanning approximately 4,000 hectares, this reserve is renowned for its rich biodiversity, encompassing grasslands, wetlands, and the Rietvlei Dam.
