FUN FACT!

Over 40% of all known human ancestor fossils on Earth have been discovered in this single area, making it one of the most important archaeological sites in the world for studying human evolution

  • SIGNIFICANCE This region is renowned for its extensive fossil record, including:
    Australopithecus africanus: Notably, the 2.3-million-year-old fossil known as "Mrs. Ples" was discovered in the Sterkfontein Caves
    "Little Foot": A nearly complete Australopithecus skeleton, estimated to be around 3.67 million years old.
    These discoveries have been pivotal in understanding the development of early hominins and their environments.
  • Maropeng Visitor Centre Maropeng serves as the official visitor center for the Cradle of Humankind. The center offers interactive exhibits that trace the journey of human evolution, featuring:​
    Fossil displays and ancient artifacts.
    Multimedia presentations on the history of life on Earth.
    Educational programs and guided tours.
    Visitors can also explore the nearby Sterkfontein Caves, where many significant fossils have been unearthed. ​

​The Cradle of Humankind is a UNESCO World Heritage Site located approximately 50 kilometers northwest of Johannesburg, South Africa. Spanning over 47,000 hectares, it is one of the world's richest hominin fossil sites, offering profound insights into human evolution.
