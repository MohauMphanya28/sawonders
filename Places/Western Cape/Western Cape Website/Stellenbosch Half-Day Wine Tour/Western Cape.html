Stellenbosch: Half-Day Wine Tour

343 Reviews

Location: Stellenbosch, Cape Town, western Cape

Indulge in the Flavors of Stellenbosch

Combine wine tastings, cheese tastings, and a cellar visit on this action-packed half-day tour of Stellenbosch. Experience some exciting wine pairings while learning about the wine production.

Prices

Shared Group Tour (small group):
R 800 – R 1,500 per person
Includes visits to 2–3 wine estates, tastings, and round-trip transport from Stellenbosch or Cape Town.

Private Tour (exclusive for your group):
R 1,500 per person (or a flat rate of R 3,500 for a private group)
More flexibility, VIP tastings, and possibly lunch.

Self-Drive & Pay-as-You-Go:
Tastings cost R 200 per winery, plus your own transport.

Premium Wine & Food Pairing Tours:
R 2,000 per person (includes gourmet food pairings at top estates).

  • Wine Tastings at Top Estates Visit 2–3 award-winning wine farms (depending on time).
    Sample a variety of wines, including Chenin Blanc, Pinotage, Cabernet Sauvignon, and Méthode Cap Classique (MCC) sparkling wines.
  • Wine & Food Pairings Enjoy wine and cheese pairings (e.g., Fairview or Simonsig).
    Try wine and chocolate pairings (like at Lanzerac or Waterford).
  • Scenic Vineyard Drives & Photo Stops Drive through the Helshoogte Pass for stunning views.
    Stop for photos among the rolling vineyards and mountain backdrops.
  • Cellar Tours & Winemaking Insights Offer short guided cellar tours (e.g., Spier or Kanonkop).
    Learn about South Africa’s winemaking history and techniques.

The Stellenbosch Half-Day Wine Tour offers a delightful introduction to South Africa’s renowned winelands, combining history, scenic beauty, and world-class wines.Founded in 1679 by Dutch Governor Simon van der Stel, Stellenbosch is the second-oldest town South Africa (after Cape Town).The fertile valleys and Mediterranean climate made it ideal for vineyards, with wine production dating back to the late century.French Huguenot refugees (late 1600s) brought advanced winemaking techniques, helping establish Stellenbosch as a premier wine region.Today, it’s home to over 200 wine farms, many with historic Cape Dutch architecture.
