Cape Town’s Marine Wildlife Tours from the V&A Waterfront have grown alongside the city’s rich maritime history. The waterfront itself, once a bustling 19th-century harbor, transformed in the 1990s into a vibrant tourism hub, sparking interest in ocean adventures. Local operators began offering seal snorkeling, whale watching, and dolphin cruises, capitalizing on the region’s diverse marine life—including Cape fur seals (which colonized the nearby Duiker Island), southern right whales (migrating close to shore), and playful dolphins. Over time, eco-tourism regulations were introduced to protect wildlife, ensuring sustainable interactions. Today, these tours blend adventure, conservation, and education, drawing visitors eager to experience Cape Town’s stunning coastal ecosystems up close.
