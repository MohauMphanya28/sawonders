Kirstenbosch National Botanical Garden, established in 1913, is one of the world's most renowned botanical gardens and the first in the world dedicated to a country's indigenous flora. Located on the eastern slopes of Table Mountain in Cape Town, South Africa, it was founded to preserve and showcase the unique Cape Floristic Region, a biodiversity hotspot. Over the years, Kirstenbosch has grown from a modest conservation project into a globally celebrated garden, attracting visitors with its stunning landscapes, rare plant species, and iconic attractions like the Centenary Tree Canopy Walkway. The garden has played a vital role in botanical research, conservation, and public education, earning recognition as a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 2004 as part of the Cape Floral Region Protected Areas. Today, entry tickets help support its ongoing mission to protect South Africa's rich natural heritage.
