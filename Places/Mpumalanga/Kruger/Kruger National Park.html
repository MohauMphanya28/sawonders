Location: Northeastern South Africa, Limpopo & Mpumalanga

Premier Wildlife Safari Destination

Kruger National Park is one of Africa's largest game reserves. It offers an unrivaled wildlife experience, with diverse landscapes and the Big Five – lion, leopard, rhinoceros, elephant, and buffalo.

  • Safari Lodges & Camps Options range from luxurious private lodges to budget-friendly camping spots across the park.
  • Wildlife Viewing Home to the Big Five and hundreds of bird species, ideal for safari drives and guided walks.
  • Diverse Ecosystems From savannahs to forests, the park features varied flora and fauna across its vast area.
  • Family-Friendly Adventures Safe, guided tours and educational activities for children and families to learn about nature.

Established in 1898, Kruger National Park is South Africa’s oldest and most iconic game reserve. It spans nearly 20,000 km² and stretches across Limpopo and Mpumalanga provinces. Visitors enjoy self-drive safaris, guided bush walks, and exceptional biodiversity, making Kruger a top destination for eco-tourism and wildlife photography.
