Blyde River Canyon

68 Reviews

Location: Mpumalanga, South Africa

Scenic Natural Wonder

The Blyde River Canyon is one of the largest canyons on Earth and the second-largest in Africa. Known for its lush subtropical foliage, dramatic cliffs, and the iconic "Three Rondavels", it's a must-see in Mpumalanga.

  • Incredible Viewpoints Visit God’s Window, Bourke’s Luck Potholes, and the Three Rondavels for jaw-dropping scenery.
  • Hiking Trails Enjoy hikes through lush landscapes, waterfalls, and river paths ideal for nature lovers.
  • Boat Cruises Take boat tours on the Blyde Dam for a unique view of the canyon walls and local wildlife.
  • Photographer’s Paradise The ever-changing light and mist offer fantastic opportunities for scenic photography.

Blyde River Canyon is a dramatic natural feature located in Mpumalanga province. It's part of the Panorama Route and is one of the most scenic places in South Africa. The canyon is lush and green, unlike the dry Fish River Canyon, and offers breathtaking viewpoints, waterfalls, and geological wonders.
