Recommended Places To Visit in Mpumalanga

Natural Wonder

Blyde River Canyon

The Blyde River Canyon is one of the largest canyons in the world and Mpumalanga's most spectacular natural attraction. Stretching 26km in length, it features breathtaking viewpoints like the Three Rondavels and Bourke's Luck Potholes, with lush subtropical foliage and dramatic rock formations.

Prices

Entrance - R50 per person

Guided tours from R300 per person

Wildlife Safari

Kruger National Park

One of Africa's largest game reserves, Kruger National Park offers an unparalleled safari experience. Home to the Big Five (lion, leopard, rhino, elephant, and buffalo), the park spans nearly 2 million hectares of diverse ecosystems with excellent infrastructure for self-drive or guided tours.

Prices

Daily conservation fee: R110 (SA residents)

Accommodation from R800 per night

Scenic Viewpoint

God's Window

Part of the Panorama Route, God's Window offers one of the most spectacular views in South Africa. On clear days, you can see over the Lowveld to the distant Lebombo Mountains on the Mozambique border. The viewpoint sits at the edge of a 700m cliff with lush indigenous forest.

Prices

Entrance - R25 per person

Adventure Destination

Graskop

The charming town of Graskop serves as the gateway to the Panorama Route. Known for its pancake houses and proximity to major attractions, it's also home to the Graskop Gorge Lift Co. - a glass elevator descending into the forest canopy with a suspension bridge and zipline adventures.

Prices

Gorge Lift - R150 per adult

Zipline - R450 per person

Historic Gold Rush Town

Pilgrim's Rest

This living museum town preserves the atmosphere of the 1870s gold rush era. All buildings are national monuments, housing museums, quaint shops, and original miner's houses. Visitors can try gold panning and learn about Mpumalanga's mining history.

Prices

Museum entry - R40 per adult

Gold panning - R80 per session

Ancient Cave System

Sudwala Caves

Dating back 240 million years, these are the oldest known caves in the world. The spectacular dolomite chambers feature fascinating rock formations and a constant temperature of 17°C. The caves have served as a refuge for ancient peoples and more recently as a secret meeting place.

Prices

Standard tour - R100 per adult

Crystal tour - R250 per adult
