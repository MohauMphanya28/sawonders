Pilanesberg National Park

Location: North West Province, near Sun City, South Africa

A wildlife paradise in an extinct volcano

Pilanesberg National Park is one of South Africa’s most accessible and scenic safari destinations. Located in a crater of an extinct volcano, the park is home to the Big Five and over 300 bird species.

Entrance Fees

Adult - R110 / day

Child - R30 / day

  • Diverse Wildlife Home to the Big Five and various antelope species in a malaria-free zone.
  • Stunning Scenery Formed inside an ancient volcanic crater, the park offers incredible geological formations.
  • Guided Safari Drives Choose from self-drives or guided game drives for an immersive safari experience.
  • Close to Sun City Perfect for combining luxury entertainment with a wildlife experience.

Pilanesberg National Park is a popular safari destination located in the North West Province of South Africa. Set in an ancient volcanic crater, the park offers a unique and accessible wilderness experience just a few hours from Johannesburg and Pretoria. With over 55,000 hectares of diverse landscapes and a rich array of wildlife including the Big Five, it’s ideal for day trips or overnight stays, especially when paired with nearby Sun City.
