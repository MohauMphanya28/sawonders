Location: Vhembe District, Limpopo Province, South Africa

Heart of Venda Culture

Thohoyandou is the vibrant capital of the Vhembe District and a cultural hub for the Venda people. It features a mix of traditional and modern attractions including markets, museums, and nearby nature reserves.

Prices

Adult - R100 / day

Child - R60 / day

  • Lively Markets Traditional markets offering handmade crafts, fresh produce, and local delicacies.
  • Natural Attractions Close to Phiphidi Waterfalls, Thathe Vondo Forest, and Nwanedi Nature Reserve.
  • Rich Cultural Experiences Learn about Venda traditions, music, dance, and visit heritage sites.
  • Eco-Tourism Opportunities Scenic nature trails and community-run eco lodges promoting sustainability.

Thohoyandou, meaning “head of the elephant,” is named after a powerful Venda king and represents the pride of the Vhembe region. Visitors can enjoy a blend of cultural richness and scenic beauty, making it a perfect destination for learning, adventure, and relaxation. From the bustling vendors to the surrounding forests and waterfalls, Thohoyandou offers a unique taste of Limpopo’s soul.
