Cradle of Human History

Makapan’s Valley is a World Heritage Site recognized for its deep anthropological and archaeological significance. It preserves the story of early humans and their environments from over 3 million years ago.

Makapan’s Valley, located near Mokopane in Limpopo, offers a rare window into early human life. This site features an extensive fossil record and is known for the historic siege of Chief Makapan in 1854. As part of South Africa’s Cradle of Humankind, the valley stands as a testament to ancient civilizations, biodiversity, and the legacy of human resilience.
