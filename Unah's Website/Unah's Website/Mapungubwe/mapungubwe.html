Mapungubwe National Park

58 Reviews

Location: At the confluence of the Limpopo and Shashe Rivers, Limpopo Province, South Africa

World Heritage Site of Cultural and Natural Wonder

Mapungubwe National Park is a UNESCO World Heritage Site, offering a glimpse into ancient African civilization, incredible landscapes, and abundant wildlife. It is home to the famous golden rhinoceros and centuries of rich history.

  • Unique Accommodation Stay at scenic lodges or campsites with views of baobabs, sandstone cliffs, and river valleys.
  • Ancient African Kingdom Discover the archaeological treasures of the Kingdom of Mapungubwe dating back to the 13th century.
  • Wildlife & Landscape See elephants, leopards, and antelope among impressive rock formations and river confluences.
  • Eco and Cultural Tours Join guided walks, heritage site visits, and experience the serenity of unspoiled nature.

Mapungubwe National Park is a remarkable place where history, culture, and nature intersect. Once the center of a powerful African kingdom, it now offers visitors the chance to explore majestic baobabs, ancient ruins, and thriving wildlife in a peaceful, scenic setting in the northern Limpopo region of South Africa.
